College Majors¶

The significance of universities has been getting higher and higher since 21st century began. Many companies want to hire competitive college students to flourish, and it's believed that knowledge students can acquire in school year makes post-graduate life vividly colorful. However, some people started questioning about the quality of college education as its populality goes up. In my opinion as a current student, it's unarguable that some students are not motivated enough to study and end up with less satisfactory jobs.

In this notebook, I'm going to explore a dataset about college majors and their jobs after graduation (the data source link here). Through data exploring, I'll find the answers of the following questions:

  • Do students in more popular majors make more money?
  • How many majors are predominantly male? Predominantly female?
  • What majors have higher median salary and lower unemployment rate?

Because knowing how much they can potentially earn in the future can be a great motivation for students to learn in university, I think this analysis is worth implementing. In the very beginning of this notebook, I posed a question that "Do students in more popular majors make more money?"
As we can see in the left two scatter plots and their correlation, we cannot say that more popular majors necessarily make more money. "Total" and "Median" correlation of -0.107 tells us that the more popular majors are, the less median salaries. This isn't an unexpected result actually because we can balance demand and supply, so companies don't have to pay high salaries which makes median goes up.

When it comes to unemployment rates, correlation of 0.083 say popularlity of majors makes no difference to it. This is very interesting result beacause that means students don't have to change their major to find a job technically (you may change your major if you want to earn more though).

Another thing we can say from the scatter plots and the correlations is that majors predominated by men generate higher salaries -- in other word, majors with more women have lower salaries unfortunately (correlation of -0.619 shows strong negative relationship between ShareWomen and Median). Although men and women have equal rights to learn in colleges, it seems like we have another new issue to deal with.

The following grouped bar charts help us understand how much ShareWomen rate negatively relates to Median salaries.

High Median Salary & Low Unemployment Rate Major Categories¶

In the previous section, we explored how popularity of majors is related to their future jobs. Now we will dive deeper into a question "Which major category is best to earn money specifically?" It totally depends on who you are what defines a good earning major, but I'd like to define it by "high median salary" and "low unemployment rate". And even though you don't really care about which major makes more money, it's worth knowing what the major is because those highly-demanding major may have significant influence on our life.
